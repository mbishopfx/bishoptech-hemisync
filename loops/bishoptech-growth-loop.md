# BishopTech.dev Growth Loop

Purpose
- Monitor Google Search Console for bishoptech.dev every 3 days.
- Find high-intent queries and page gaps that can become service pages, case studies, blog posts, and social angles.
- Focus on the business outcomes BishopTech.dev is trying to attract: builds, prototypes, debugging, hosting, management, and rescue work for abandoned or vibe-coded projects.

Cadence
- Run every 3 days.
- If data is sparse, keep the watchlist and do not invent a trend.

Target audience
- Small businesses that need a build partner.
- Entrepreneurs who need prototypes or MVPs.
- Founders with a site, app, or system that broke after the original developer disappeared.
- Teams that need a calmer, more reliable technical partner to finish, fix, host, and manage the thing they already started.

What to look for
- Queries about fixing broken websites, rescuing abandoned projects, modernizing outdated builds, or getting an MVP production-ready.
- Queries with high impressions and weak CTR.
- Rising service-intent phrases that indicate someone wants a quote, call, consultation, or rescue plan.
- Content gaps that can be filled by a premium service page or a practical blog post.

Decision rules
- Prefer pages that speak to buyer intent and conversion.
- Keep the writing concrete and useful.
- Avoid generic agency language that does not map to a real problem.
- Use the existing BishopTech.dev visual theme and HTML structure when turning a candidate into a page.

Publishing workflow
- Draft the page in the existing site theme.
- Keep HTML semantic and clean: headings, short sections, bullet lists, CTAs, and FAQ blocks where useful.
- Add schema/internal links when appropriate.
- Make sure the page is included in the sitemap.
- Submit the URL for indexing after deployment.
- Record the result in loops/bishoptech-growth-state.md.
